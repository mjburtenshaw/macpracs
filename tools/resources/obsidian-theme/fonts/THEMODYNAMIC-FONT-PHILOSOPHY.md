# Themodynamic Stark - Font Philosophy

> *"The system does not seek peace. It seeks containment."*

## The Attack on Titan Aesthetic

The visual inspiration from **Attack on Titan** suggests:

- **Brutal functionality** over decoration
- **Military/utilitarian** aesthetic - field reports, not literature
- **Stark, industrial** world with rare moments of beauty
- **Germanic/technical** influences (die Mauer, Survey Corps logs)
- **Archival precision** - witness and document, don't embellish
- **High contrast** between mundane utility and meaningful moments

## Themodynamic Stark Principle

> *"Color is rare. When it appears, it means something."*

Applied to typography:
- **Most text is functional** - the desaturated base
- **Distinction is earned** - like pops of color in the palette
- **Not about prettiness** - about containment and witness
- **Archival, not literary** - Colt's Oswikaric field reports, not poetry

## Font Philosophy Options

### Option 1: The Field Report (Pure Utilitarian)

**All contexts use variants of the SAME typeface family.**

Like a military organization that standardizes on ONE font for all official documents.

**IBM Plex Family:**
- **Prose:** IBM Plex Serif (technical serif, not warm)
- **Notes:** IBM Plex Sans (systematic, industrial)
- **Code:** IBM Plex Mono (obvious choice)

**Rationale:**
- Created by IBM for technical documentation
- Systematic, not humanist
- Germanic precision meets industrial design
- All three variants designed together
- Free and open source
- "Big Blue" institutional authority

**Vibe:** "Official Survey Corps documentation system"

---

### Option 2: The Typewriter (Maximum Brutality)

**Everything is monospace.** The ultimate field report aesthetic.

**Courier Variants:**
- **Prose:** Courier Prime (refined version of Courier)
- **Notes:** Courier Prime (same)
- **Code:** Courier Prime Code (optimized for programming)

**Rationale:**
- Actual typewriter font - field reports, telegraph messages
- Zero pretension - pure functionality
- Monospace everywhere = maximum uniformity
- Like military logs typed on portable typewriters
- The font that documented wars and expeditions

**Vibe:** "Survey Corps field reports typed in the field"

---

### Option 3: The German Standard (Industrial Precision)

**DIN-inspired systematic fonts.**

**The System:**
- **Prose:** Roboto Slab (geometric slab serif, industrial)
- **Notes:** DIN Next or Roboto (German road sign aesthetic)
- **Code:** Roboto Mono (mechanical precision)

**Rationale:**
- DIN = Deutsche Industrie Norm (German Industrial Standard)
- Used on road signs, technical documents
- Geometric, not humanist
- Maximum legibility with zero warmth
- The font of engineering blueprints

**Vibe:** "German military technical documentation"

---

### Option 4: The Archive (Stark Contrast)

**Monospace base with slab serif for rare distinction.**

Like the Themodynamic palette: desaturated base, pops of meaningful color.

**The System:**
- **Prose:** Zilla Slab (Mozilla's utilitarian slab serif) - **the rare distinction**
- **Notes:** Anonymous Pro (monospace sans) - **the functional base**
- **Code:** Anonymous Pro (same) - **pure utility**

**Rationale:**
- Most text is monospace (desaturated)
- Prose files get slab serif distinction (pop of color)
- Anonymous Pro: designed for coders who read a lot
- Zilla Slab: utilitarian, not decorative
- High contrast between modes = meaningful difference

**Vibe:** "Field reports (mono) vs. formal chronicles (slab)"

## Recommended: Option 1 (IBM Plex Family)

### Why IBM Plex Aligns Best

1. **Systematic Unity**
   - One family, three variants
   - Like a standardized military documentation system
   - Visual consistency with contextual distinction

2. **Technical Authority**
   - Created for IBM's rebranding
   - Designed for technical content
   - Industrial, not literary

3. **Germanic Precision**
   - Influenced by Helvetica and Akzidenz-Grotesk
   - Systematic, not humanist
   - Engineering precision

4. **Archival Quality**
   - Designed for longevity
   - Clear at all sizes
   - Professional, institutional

5. **Attack on Titan Aesthetic**
   - Could imagine Survey Corps using this
   - Technical reports, not novels
   - Functional, not decorative
   - Military precision

### The IBM Plex System

**IBM Plex Serif** (Prose):
- Technical serif, not warm/literary
- Slab-like qualities without being fully slab
- Designed for body text in technical documentation
- Has authority without pretension

**IBM Plex Sans** (Notes):
- Systematic, geometric
- Industrial without being cold
- Excellent for scanning and hierarchies
- The workhorse font

**IBM Plex Mono** (Code):
- Designed for programming
- Clear, unambiguous characters
- Good ligature support
- Technical precision
